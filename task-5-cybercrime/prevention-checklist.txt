Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist

-Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Always enable 2FA for your college email and social media accounts by using Google Authenticator or SMS.


-Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Never use the password "123456" or your own name. Always ensure to have a combination of capital and small case letters, numbers, and symbols for different accounts.


-Verify Links Before Clicking: Always hover over the links in the messages or emails you have received and check the actual URL. Never click on the links sent by unknown people.


-Update Software Regularly: Always keep your operating system and antivirus updated to protect yourself from the latest hacking techniques used by cyber criminals.


-Be Skeptical of "Urgent" Requests: Always be aware of the messages or emails you have received. If the messages or emails you have received scare you by saying things like "your account will be deleted in 2 hours," then it is a sure shot scam.


-Review App Permissions: Always check the app permissions on your smartphone and ensure that the apps you have installed do not use the camera, microphone, and contact list in any way.


-Financial Safety - UPI PIN Logic: You should never reveal your UPI PIN for receiving money through UPI. You only need a UPI PIN when you are sending money.

-Financial Safety - Verify Merchant Names: Before clicking on 'Pay' on a UPI payment application, one has to re-verify the verified name of the recipient.
